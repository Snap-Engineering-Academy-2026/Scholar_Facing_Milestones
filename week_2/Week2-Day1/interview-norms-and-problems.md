# Norms Considerations

Discuss these with your group before proceeding!

 - Most importantly, how can you be supportive? 
 - In some coding interviews, interviewers will occasionally speak or ask questions. Do you want this? If so, when/what kind?
 - You will probably get stuck at some point during the problem. What should your group do? (Remember, some amount of struggle is productive.)
 - How will you manage time for interviews and feedback?
 - Different people will have different answers!

# Problems

This week we have a few "groupings" for the problems. We have recommended problems and challenge problems, and then we also noted some of the other concepts the problems emphasize. 

## Recommended Problems

1. Arrays - [Find the Peaks](https://leetcode.com/problems/find-the-peaks/description/) - _This is a good problem, but the wording is funky so make sure you ask questions before you start coding_
2. Hashmaps - [Roman to Integer](https://leetcode.com/problems/roman-to-integer/) (**must use hashmap to solve**)
3. Hashmaps - [Sum of Unique Elements](https://leetcode.com/problems/sum-of-unique-elements/) (**must use hashmap to solve**)
4. Arrays - [Remove Duplicates from Sorted Array](https://leetcode.com/problems/remove-duplicates-from-sorted-array/)
5. Big O, Arrays - [Best Time to Buy and Sell Stock](https://leetcode.com/problems/best-time-to-buy-and-sell-stock/description/) - _Usually you will need to optimize your code after you initially solve the problem_ 
6. Big O, Arrays, Hashmaps - [How Many Numbers Are Smaller Than the Current Number](https://leetcode.com/problems/how-many-numbers-are-smaller-than-the-current-number/) - _Usually you will need to optimize your code after you initially solve the problem_
